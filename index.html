Toot-In-Common

Central PA Flute Choir - State College, PA

About Us

After returning from a tour of the Baltic States with the International Flute Orchestra, Judy Moore was inspired to form a flute choir in State College, PA. Toot-in-Common is the result. Flute players from central Pennsylvania meet weekly in preparation for concerts. Our literature consists of popular and classical favorites arranged for flute choir and works written especially for flute choir. Instrumentation includes 1 piccolo, 6-8 C flutes, 2 alto flutes, 1 bass flute, and 1 contrabass flute. Toot-in-Common has performed at State College First Night, at The People’s Choice Festival, State College Rotary, St. Andrew’s Episcopal Church in State College, Trinity Lutheran Church in State College, and Bellefonte’s Victorian Christmas.

2019 Holiday Concert

The Central Pennsylvania Flute Choir Toot-in-Common will perform a Holiday Concert, Monday, December 16 at 7:30 in the sanctuary of Saint Andrew’s Episcopal Church, 208 West Foster Avenue, State College. The group plays works composed especially for flute choir, a grouping of regular C flutes, piccolo, alto flute, bass flute and contrabass flute. We welcome you to hear a funky version of “God Rest Ye Merry Gentlemen”; “Nimrod”, from Elgar’s Enigma Variations; “Fire & Ice”, an imaginative work in three movements including Sky Fire, Secrets, and Snow Mist; and a choral work by John Rutter adapted for flute choir. Please join us at St. Andrew’s Episcopal Church on December 16 at 7:30 for this free offering from talented flutists. A reception will follow with a Good Will Offering.
